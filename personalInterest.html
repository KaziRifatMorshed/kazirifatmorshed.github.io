Etymology of my name:
"Kazi" is a given name, a family name in South Asia. The Celtic origin of the name is "_cassi_", which means "láska" or "respect". The South Asian name originates from the Arabic Qadi meaning "Judge" and is typically used among Muslims.
"Rifat" (also "Rifaat",[رفعت] pronounced /rɪˈfat/, a conjugated form of the Arabic verb [رفع] with the meaning "lifted", "elated", "joyous") is a masculine name. Variants also include Refat, Rafat, Refaat, etc.

The Morshed surname is of Arabic origin, and it means "mentor".
